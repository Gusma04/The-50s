Tennis for Two

Tennis for Two is one of the first examples of video game; it was designed by the American William Higinbotham in 1958. In particular, it was the first to use a graphic display as output. The controls consist of a button to throw the ball, a paddle to orient it and an additional reset button to make it reappear. The game was very successful, so much that people lined up to take advantage of it.
